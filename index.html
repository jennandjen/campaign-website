KEY ISSUES

Jenn Klipp

  • Insuring safety for students, staff, and parents during (and beyond) the COVID-19 pandemic
  • Developing and expanding educational collaborations with area businesses, organizations, Northern Michigan University, and Michigan Technological University
  • Updating building facilities to ensure student and staff physical safety and create 21st century learning spaces for all MAPS children, educators and staff through responsible financial investment
  • Preparing all MAPS students for a 21st century world through the support of integrated teaching and technology in a global environment

Jenn Ray

  • I am passionate about the development, education and success of all children in our school system, recognizing diverse learning styles and needs in the classroom.
  • Investing in the infrastructure and the technology our students need in order to meet the challenges of the 21st century is clearly both a necessity and a priority.
  • As a business owner confronted with the challenges of COVID, I am aware of the need for adaptive, flexible, informed decision making in order to best support our administrators, educators, and children.
  • I value the input of others. During these trying times when the board must navigate tricky issues like reopening schools, mask mandates, and virtual learning, it will be important to ensure teachers, staff, parents, and students feel heard.

  • As we become more acutely aware of personal biases and a long history of oppression in all of our social systems, I am committed to ensuring that all schools are engaged in ongoing cultural competency and implicit bias training.
  • It’s all about the kids!” This has always been my mantra. I am passionate about elevating the voices and leadership of the youth themselves. They are gifted, articulate, and capable in being part of the decision making process.
MEET JENN KLIPP
Jenn Klipp Family

Jenn Klipp was born and raised in Flint, Michigan. After graduating from Kearsley High School in 1991, she received her Bachelor of Art in English Literature from Hillsdale College, and has since taken dozens of graduate level courses concentrating on Educational Leadership and Curriculum Development and Design from Eastern Michigan University and NMU. After graduation, Jenn traveled extensively in the US and abroad before landing in the Keweenaw where she taught at Lake Linden-Hubbell schools for four years before returning to Flint with her husband. She taught at Flint Beecher for 6 years, and while there she designed and implemented the only board-certified Advanced Placement curriculum in literature in an urban school district in the State of Michigan. When her husband, Taylor, was offered the opportunity to establish a new office in the upper Great Lakes region, they gratefully moved back to the Upper Peninsula with their daughter, Lotus. In 2013, Jenn took a teaching position in the Ishpeming school district where she has been teaching for over 7 years. Throughout her career, Jenn has served in various leadership roles, most notably spearheading a committee that partnered with the State of Michigan to bring a district out of financial insolvency. Jenn has developed original curriculum in her districts, including a Humanities curriculum that was recognized by the State of Michigan in its Teaching Excellence initiative in 2017.

Since moving to Marquette in 2009, Jenn and her husband, Taylor, have been engaged in local and regional community service, from serving in local city commissions and boards, to charitable work with their church, to volunteering in various positions that serve people in poverty. Their daughter, Lotus, is now starting her first year at Bothwell Middle School. Jenn recognizes the excellence already present in the MAPS system, and is anxious to help it strengthen and grow. Jenn knows that for schools and families to effectively work together for their children, parents and students must feel respected and valued. She is committed to help MAPS provide a welcoming and inclusive environment for all students, regardless of socio-economic status, race, gender, or religion. In order to ensure this, Jenn recognizes that decision-makers must be brave enough follow an evidence-based, data-driven approach that fosters a healthy school culture and promotes student well-being.

MEET JEN RAY
Jen Ray Family

Jen grew up in Evanston, IL and was transplanted to Marquette, Michigan her senior year of high school, graduating from MSHS in 1983. She then attended the University of Michigan, during which she took a number of gap years to travel and study Spanish and French. Jen put herself through college by working multiple jobs and creating side businesses, which enabled her to finish her degree in Political Science while living in Chicago.

After working in the political consulting and non-profit arenas for a number of years, Jen chose to stay home with her three kids. The desire to raise their kids in a small, Midwest town brought Jen and her husband, Tom Vear, back to Marquette in 2001. Jen proceeded to home school her kids until they each decided to attend public school at Marquette Senior High School. In 2007, Jen and Tom refurbished and re-opened Donckers. Eight years later, they opened the Delft Bistro. Jen has a demonstrated ability to focus on fine details while keeping sight of a bigger picture. Her passion has always been a combination of helping people and creating an environment where everyone feels welcome. She is passionate about the community of Marquette and is dedicated to working hard to ensure MAPS continues to provide an exceptional nurturing learning environment for children in our community.
